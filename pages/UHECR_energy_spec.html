Introduction

I spent 5 years at the University of Utah conducting research with the Telescope Array Cosmic Ray Observatory with Charles Jui as my thesis advisor. I am currently editing my thesis for my Ph.D. in Physics on the Ultra-High Energy Cosmic Ray energy spectrum (UHECR). My thesis focuses on collecting, processing, and reconstructing data of the new Telescope Array expansion (TAx4) using a hybrid analysis. Hybrid analysis utilizes information from both Surface Detector (SD) and Flourescence Detector (FD) to constrain the reconstructed shower's "Timing verse Angle" fit. My analysis has yielded the first TAx4 hybrid cosmic ray energy spectrum. I also report the performance of TAx4 and present data-MC comparisions.

Abstract

The Telescope Array (TA) Cosmic Ray Observatory, located in Millard County, Utah, is the largest cosmic ray detector in the Northern hemisphere. Following the evidence for a hotspot in the arrival directions of the highest energy cosmic rays, TA underwent the TAx4 upgrade to expand the area of our Surface

Detectors (SD) by a factor of 4 and add new Fluorescence Detector (FD) stations to view over the new SD arrays. The new fluorescence telescopes are comprised of refurbished and upgraded detectors from the HiRes-II experiment. Currently, TAx4 consists of 12 FDs and 257 SDs, of a planned 500, at a spacing of 2.08 km spread over two sites. TAx4 North (4 FDs), completed in 2018, views over the northern wing of SDs, and TAx4 South (8 FDs), completed in 2019, views over the southern wing. In this dissertation, we calculate a preliminary energy spectrum using hybrid analysis on two years of data and report the performance of the TAx4 FDs, showing detector resolutions and data-MC comparisons. This preliminary hybrid energy spectrum is in agreement previous TA measurements.

Monte Carlo

Complex Monte Carlo (MC) simulations are used to determine the detectors responce to cosmic ray events, detector aperture, and determine detector resolutions. The MC used in this analysis was generated using QGSJetII-03 protons. The MC is constructed in such a way that it is indistingishable from the data, then the same reconstruction programs that are used on the data are used on the MC. The validity of the MC can then be determined by comparing distributions of observables produced in the data.
